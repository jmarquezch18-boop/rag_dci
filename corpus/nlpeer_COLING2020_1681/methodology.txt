We use a transformer model for all experiments and train a sentence-level model as a baseline.

The context-aware model in our experimental setup is a concatenation model (Tiedemann and Scherrer, 2017) (concat) which is trained on a concatenation of consecutive sentences.

concat is a standard transformer model and it differs from the sentence-level model only in the way that the training data is supplied to it.

The training examples for this model are modified by prepending the previous source and target sentence to the main source and target sentence, respectively.

The previous sentence is separated from the main sentence with a special token <SEP>, on both the source and target side.

This also applies to how we prepare the ContraPro and Contracat data.

We train the concatenation model on OpenSubtitles2018 data prepared in this way.

We remove documents overlapping with ContraPro.

Preprocessing details and model hyper-parameters are presented in the Appendix.
