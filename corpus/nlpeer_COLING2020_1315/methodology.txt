Three models were trained for main statement sentence classification: bag-of-words based Linear SVM, BERT (Devlin et al., 2018), and BioBERT (Lee et al., 2019).

We hypothesized that BioBERT (Lee et al., 2019) would perform the best, since it is a domain specific language representation model that is based on BERT and further trained on large-scale biomedical corpora.

BioBERT has shown to outperform BERT on three representative biomedical text mining tasks, including text classification (Lee et al., 2019).

All models were evaluated on our annotated corpus via 5-fold stratified cross-validation.

As shown in Table 3(a), BioBERT achieved a macro-averaged F1 score of 0.868, outperforming BERT and Lin-earSVM, whose scores are 0.844 and 0.732 respectively.
