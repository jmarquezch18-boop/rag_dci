We use the Random Forest algorithm (RF), an ensemble learning method, as our classifier in this study.

The classification is based on the results achieved from the myriad of decision trees it generates while training (Nayak and Natarajan, 2016;Biau, 2012).

We employ RF because it also reliably computes the permutation importance of the variables while training the classification models.

Afterwards, we use two automatic feature selection methods to assess the features used in each model: "Rank Features by Importance" [henceforth RFI] and "Sequential Forward Search" [henceforth SFS].

These methods will be detailed in subsection 4.3 and subsection 4.4.
