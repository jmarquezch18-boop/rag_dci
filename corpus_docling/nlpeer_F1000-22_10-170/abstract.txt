Background: Since the emergence of the COVID-19 outbreak, Government of Bangladesh (GoB) has taken various measures to restrict virus transmission and inform the people of the situation.

However, the success of such measures largely depends on a positive public perception of the government's ability to act decisively and the transparency of its communication.

We explored public perceptions of pandemic management efforts by the Bangladeshi health sector decision-makers in this study.

Methods: As this qualitative research was conducted during the COVID-19 pandemic, data was gathered through seven online mixedgender focus group discussions involving 50 purposively selected clinicians and non-clinicians.
