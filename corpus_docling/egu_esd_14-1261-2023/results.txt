The evaluation of temperature evolution of an oscillating water body is analysed in a numerical wave flume environment.

This approach provides a basis for the straightforward verification of the major outcome of the modelling procedures, which are presented in the previous section, against measurements in the real laboratory.

On the other hand, the numerical model may be modified to cover pseudo-random ocean waves in a large periodic domain (see e.g. Paprota, 2019) to analyse the wave-induced vertical mixing processes in offshore conditions.
